Face to Face with My Inspiration: Defending Against the Hacker I Aspire to Become

Introduction: An Unlikely Encounter

What do you do when you come face to face with the kind of hacker you dream of becoming—but you’re on the opposite side of the battle? Recently, as a Teleservice Executive and a budding cybersecurity enthusiast, I found myself in a position I never expected. I was defending my company against a skilled attacker whose tactics mirrored the same cybersecurity skills I aspire to master.

Working at a legitimate business using open-source software like ViciDial and X-Lite, I knew we were vulnerable. But this particular day’s encounter forced me into a real-time defense scenario, a wake-up call about what it means to be on the front lines of cybersecurity.

The Attack: Facing a Skilled Opponent

Our usual outbound campaign had just been switched to an inbound process. It was a simple change—or so I thought. Shortly after we started, I realized that someone was attempting to gain access to our systems. After a few suspicious calls, I quickly recognized I wasn’t dealing with a typical caller. The person on the other end was a scam baiter, intercepting and manipulating calls with the intent to compromise our data.

As an Information Technology student fascinated by hacking, I found myself in awe of this individual’s skills. He was doing exactly what I hoped to do one day: spotting vulnerabilities, controlling cyber environments, and exploiting weaknesses. But this admiration clashed with my responsibility to my company. Despite my fascination, I knew I couldn’t let him breach our defenses.

Recognizing the Risks: The Cost of Vulnerabilities

I quickly realized the potential danger if this attacker succeeded. Our company held a lot of sensitive client data—transaction details and pharmaceutical records worth millions. With this data, a skilled hacker could cause irreparable harm. I suspected that he might have exploited either the phone number we were using for the inbound calls or vulnerabilities in the open-source tools we relied on daily.

This was more than a theoretical exercise; it was a serious security incident, and I was the first line of defense.

Taking Action: My First Real-Life Incident Response

My initial reaction was to use social engineering. I tried reasoning with him, explaining that we were a legitimate business and persuading him to stop. However, he didn’t believe me, dismissing my attempts to talk things out. Realizing that diplomacy wouldn’t work, I knew I needed to act quickly to protect our systems.

I alerted my manager and team leader, urging them to exit the campaign immediately to prevent further exposure. Shutting down the inbound calls was the best way to stop this scam baiter from gaining any more access to our network. Although I’m a beginner, I relied on what I knew about basic incident response: contain the threat, limit exposure, and communicate the risk to others.

This experience showed me that, even without formal incident response training, staying calm and acting decisively can be the difference between a close call and a security breach.

Lessons Learned: Insights Gained from Facing a Real Threat

Looking back, this encounter taught me several critical lessons about cybersecurity. First, I learned the importance of quick thinking and adaptability. The scam baiter forced me to think on my feet and make decisions that could protect the company’s data. Additionally, I realized how vulnerable open-source software can be without proper security measures, highlighting the importance of understanding the tools we rely on.

On a deeper level, the experience gave me a taste of the ethical tension in cybersecurity. As much as I admired this hacker’s skill, I was reminded that the power to hack comes with great responsibility. My fascination with hacking isn’t just about finding vulnerabilities; it’s about protecting and defending against those who would exploit them.

The Road Ahead: From Cybersecurity Novice to Defender

This experience has only deepened my commitment to becoming a cybersecurity professional. I want to learn more about the vulnerabilities in systems like ViciDial, improve my understanding of defensive strategies, and eventually gain enough expertise to become a red teamer or a blue teamer myself.

I also understand that I have a lot to learn. From incident response techniques to ethical hacking methodologies, I’m eager to build on this experience. I hope to one day protect other businesses and individuals from the same kind of threats I faced that day.

Conclusion: A New Perspective on Cybersecurity

While the encounter was challenging, it solidified my passion for cybersecurity and the path I want to take. It taught me that cybersecurity isn’t just about hacking—it’s about defending and making a difference. This incident reminded me that I want to be more than just a hacker; I want to be a defender and an ethical professional in this field.

I may still be at the beginning of my journey, but thanks to this experience, I’m more motivated than ever. And who knows—one day, I hope to inspire other young hackers to use their skills for good, just as this hacker unknowingly inspired me to stay committed to my goals.
